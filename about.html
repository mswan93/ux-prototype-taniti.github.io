About Taniti

Taniti is a tropical Pacific island with white-sand beaches, rainforest, a safe harbor, a small active volcano, and a warm island culture that welcomes visitors from around the world.

Island Overview

Taniti is small enough to explore in a short stay, yet varied enough to support repeated visits. Travelers enjoy coastal scenery, mountain views, local neighborhoods, and a growing collection of tourist-friendly services.

Culture & Lifestyle

Tanitians are proud of their traditions, food, music, and craftsmanship. Visitors will find a mix of local character and modern hospitality in the island’s towns, resorts, restaurants, and tour experiences.

Visitor Highlights

Population

About 20,000 residents

Landscape

Beaches, rainforest, harbor, and volcanic terrain

Best For

Honeymoons, weddings, family trips, and getaways

Atmosphere

Relaxed, scenic, and increasingly modern

Helpful facts for first-time visitors

Taniti uses the U.S. dollar, many businesses accept cards, and English is common in tourist areas. Visitors should also plan around local holidays, when some businesses may close.
